What are the disparities within early childhood, education, and income, across race and sex in United States?

We looked at 4 different groups of people in the United States: black men, black women, white men, and white women. Using My Brotherâ€™s Keeper dataset, we captured a snapshot of the lives of people in each category.

Household Environments

If you chose 25 kids at random from each of the four categories, here's what each of their household environments would probably look like.

Insights:

1. Across all groups, mother-only households are vastly more common than father-only households.

2. Of the children with at least one parent, black children (green and blue groups) are twice as likely to be living in single-parent households compared to white children.

K-12 Education

Here's how students from each category are performing in school, on average. In both the graphs, the gray line depicts the average student's performance.

Insights:

1. Both white females and males perform above the average national score. Both black females and males perform below the average national score.

2. Score trends for both reading and math are the same for all 4 groups. From 4th to 8th grade scores increase and decrease considerably for 12th grade.

3. In all groups, the reading scores increase with grade level, whereas for math, the scores drop after 8th grade.

Higher Education

Here's how educational attainment differs by gender and race.

Insight:

1. White females are the most likely group to receive higher education after high school followed by white males, black females and black males.

Income

In the long run, income increases with educational attainment for all groups.

Insight:

1. For the population over age 25, higher levels of education can influence median annual income for individuals. The biggest jump occurs between the group which has an associateâ€™s degree and a bachelorâ€™s degree with a 49% increase.

But educational attainment doesn't necessarily lead to higher income in the short term. Here's how much money young adults from these groups are likely to make.

Insights:

1. Among those whose highest education attainment is high school, the largest income gap is between white males and black males.

2. Among those with some college level educational attainment, the largest income gap is between white males and black females.

3. Among 20-24 year olds, median annual income increases for black males who have some college-level education. Whereas, for black females, median annual income decreases with educational attainment.

4. For the same level of educational attainment with high school, white females and black females had similar median annual earnings. However, for the same level of educational attainment with college, black females make 40% less than white females.

Unemployment

In the long run, income increases with educational attainment for all groups.

Insights:

1. Black males have the highest unemployment rate, at 28%.

2. Unemployment rates for black men were twice the national average, and a little more than twice the rate for white men.

3. Unemployment rates for black women were twice the rate for white women.
